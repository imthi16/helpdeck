# GrindPro Burr Grinder — Owner's Manual

## Grind settings

The Northwind GrindPro has 40 grind settings. Settings 1–10 are fine grinds
for espresso, 11–25 are medium grinds for drip and pour-over, and 26–40 are
coarse grinds for French press and cold brew. Start espresso at setting 6 and
adjust one step at a time; never adjust the setting while the burrs are
spinning under load with an empty hopper.

## Dosing

The GrindPro doses by time. Hold the front button to grind continuously, or
tap it once for a single dose. The default single dose is 9 seconds, which
yields roughly 18 grams at a medium setting. You can change the single-dose
time by holding both the front button and the hopper lock for three seconds,
then turning the selector.

## Cleaning the burrs

Clean the burrs every two weeks with regular use. Empty the hopper, run the
grinder empty for five seconds, then remove the hopper by turning it
counter-clockwise. Lift out the upper burr using the two finger tabs and brush
both burrs with the included stiff brush. Never wash the burrs with water —
moisture causes clumping and rust. Grinder cleaning tablets are safe to use
monthly.

## Replacing the burrs

The GrindPro's conical steel burrs last approximately 500 pounds of coffee.
Replacement burr sets are available in Parts & Accessories and take about ten
minutes to install with the included hex key. Replacing burrs does not void
the warranty as long as you use genuine Northwind parts.

## Static and mess

A few sprays of water on the beans before grinding (the "Ross droplet
technique", at most one spray per 20 grams) dramatically reduces static cling.
Always use the anti-static knock before removing the grounds cup.

## GrindPro specifications

The GrindPro measures 13 cm wide by 31 cm tall, weighs 3.2 kg, and has a
250-gram bean hopper. The motor runs at 1,400 RPM and is rated for 15 minutes
of continuous duty. It carries the same 2-year warranty as our espresso
machines.

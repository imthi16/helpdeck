# Getting Started with the Brewline Espresso Machine

## Unboxing and first setup

Carefully remove the Brewline machine and accessories from the box. You should
find the machine, a portafilter with one single and one double basket, a 58mm
tamper, a cleaning brush, and a water hardness test strip. Remove all protective
film and packaging before first use.

## Filling the water tank

Lift the lid at the back of the machine and remove the water tank. Rinse it,
then fill it with fresh, cold water up to the MAX line. Do not use distilled
water, as the machine needs some minerals to measure water level correctly.
Seat the tank firmly so the valve engages.

## First power-on and priming

Turn on the machine using the power switch on the right side and wait for the
temperature light to stop blinking, which takes about 45 seconds. Before your
first coffee, run a full tank of water through the group head and steam wand to
prime and rinse the internal lines.

## Pulling your first shot

Grind about 18 grams of coffee into the double basket, distribute evenly, and
tamp level with firm pressure. Lock the portafilter into the group head, place
your cup underneath, and press the brew button. A good double shot pours in 25
to 30 seconds.

## Setting water hardness

Dip the included test strip in your water for one second and compare it to the
chart in the manual. Set the water hardness level in the machine menu so it can
remind you to descale at the right interval. Harder water needs more frequent
descaling.

## Steaming milk

Purge the steam wand for one second to clear condensation, then submerge the tip
just below the surface of cold milk. Position the pitcher so the milk spins in a
whirlpool. Steam until the pitcher is too hot to hold comfortably, about 140 to
150 degrees Fahrenheit, then wipe the wand immediately.

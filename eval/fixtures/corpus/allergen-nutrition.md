# Allergens, Ingredients & Nutrition

## What is in the bag

Every Northwind coffee bag contains exactly one ingredient: 100% arabica
coffee beans. We do not sell flavored coffees, and we never add oils,
flavorings, or preservatives. Roast date, origin, and lot number are printed
on the side of every bag.

## Allergen handling in our roastery

Our Portland roastery processes coffee only. The facility does not handle
nuts, dairy, gluten, soy, or any of the other major allergens, so
cross-contact with allergens is not a risk in the beans we roast. Our
packaging line is also coffee-only.

## Caffeine content

As a rule of thumb, a double espresso made with 18 grams of our medium roast
contains roughly 120–140 mg of caffeine, and an 8-ounce drip cup contains
roughly 90–110 mg. Lighter roasts contain marginally more caffeine by weight
than darker roasts. These are estimates — caffeine varies by origin and
extraction.

## Decaf

Our decaf line is decaffeinated using the Swiss Water Process, which uses
only water — no chemical solvents. Swiss Water decaf retains the origin
character of the bean and removes at least 99.9% of caffeine. Decaf is
roasted on the same equipment as our regular coffees.

## Dietary certifications

All Northwind coffees are certified kosher, and coffee is naturally vegan
and gluten-free. We do not currently carry an organic certification for
every lot; lots that are certified organic say so on the bag and on the
product page.

## Storage and freshness

Coffee is best within six weeks of the roast date printed on the bag. Store
beans in the resealed bag or an airtight container, away from light and
heat. Do not refrigerate beans; the freezer is acceptable only for unopened
bags stored longer than six weeks.

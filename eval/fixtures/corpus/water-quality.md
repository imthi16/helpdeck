# Water Quality Guide for the Brewline

## Why water matters

Espresso is over 90% water, and scale from hard water is the single most
common cause of Brewline service calls. Using the right water protects the
boiler, improves taste, and keeps your warranty fully intact.

## Recommended water specification

The Brewline performs best with total hardness between 50 and 100 ppm (3–6
grains per gallon) and a pH between 6.5 and 8.0. Water above 150 ppm total
hardness requires descaling twice as often. Never use fully distilled or
reverse-osmosis water on its own — completely mineral-free water is corrosive
to the boiler and prevents the water-level sensor from working reliably.

## The Northwind water filter

The Brewline's water tank accepts the Northwind in-tank softening filter,
which reduces hardness by roughly 60%. Replace the filter every two months,
or every month if your tap water is harder than 150 ppm. The machine does not
track the filter — set a reminder when you install one. Filters are sold in
three-packs in Parts & Accessories.

## Testing your water

Every new Brewline ships with two hardness test strips in the accessories
box. Dip a strip in your tap water for one second and compare it to the
printed scale after 15 seconds. Strips are also available free on request
with any order — add a note at checkout.

## Well water and softened water

Well water varies too much to give a general rule — test it before first
use. Water from a sodium ion-exchange home softener reads as soft but its
sodium content can still corrode the boiler over time; we recommend blending
softened water 50/50 with unsoftened tap water, or using bottled spring
water within the recommended hardness range.

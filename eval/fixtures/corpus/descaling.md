# Cleaning & Descaling

## Why descaling matters

Over time, minerals in your water leave limescale deposits inside the boiler and
pipes of your espresso machine. Limescale reduces heating efficiency, weakens
steam pressure, and can eventually damage the machine. Regular descaling keeps
the Brewline running well and protects your warranty.

## How often to descale

Descale the machine every three months with normal use and medium-hardness
water. If you have hard water or use the machine several times a day, descale
every four to six weeks. The machine will show a descaling reminder based on the
water hardness setting you entered during setup.

## What to use

Use only Northwind food-safe descaling solution or another citric-acid-based
descaler made for espresso machines. Never use vinegar, which leaves a lingering
taste, and never use harsh chemical descalers, which can corrode internal parts
and void your warranty.

## Descaling procedure

Empty the water tank and mix one part descaling solution with the amount of
water listed on the bottle. Pour the mixture into the tank. Run one third of the
tank through the group head, one third through the steam wand, and let the
machine sit for 15 minutes. Then run the rest through both outlets.

## Rinsing after descaling

After descaling, remove the tank, rinse it thoroughly, and refill it with fresh
water. Run two full tanks of clean water through the group head and steam wand
to remove any remaining descaling solution before making coffee again.

## Daily and weekly cleaning

After each session, wipe the steam wand and purge it, and knock out the used
puck. Once a week, backflush the machine with plain water using the blind
basket, and soak the portafilter and baskets in warm water with a little group
head cleaner to remove coffee oils.

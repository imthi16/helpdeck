# Troubleshooting Common Problems

## The machine will not turn on

First, confirm the power cable is fully seated in both the machine and the wall
outlet, and try a different outlet. If the machine still shows no lights, check
the fuse in the plug where applicable. If none of this helps, the thermal
cutout may have tripped; let the machine cool for 30 minutes and try again.

## Coffee pours too fast and tastes weak

A fast, weak shot usually means the grind is too coarse or the dose is too low.
Grind finer and increase the dose slightly so the puck offers more resistance.
Aim for a double shot that pours in 25 to 30 seconds. Also make sure you are
tamping level and with firm, consistent pressure.

## Coffee pours too slow or not at all

If almost no coffee comes out, the grind is likely too fine or the basket is
overfilled, choking the machine. Grind coarser and reduce the dose. If the
problem continues with fresh grounds, the shower screen may be clogged and need
cleaning or a backflush.

## No water or weak pump pressure

If the pump runs loudly but little water comes through, the machine may be
airlocked. Run water through the steam wand with the tank full to reprime the
pump. Check that the water tank valve is seated and that the tank is not empty.

## The steam wand produces little or no steam

Weak steam is often caused by a blocked steam tip. Turn off and cool the
machine, then use the pin on the cleaning tool to clear each hole in the tip. A
machine that has not been descaled in a long time can also lose steam power due
to limescale.

## Error light is blinking

A continuously blinking temperature light after several minutes indicates a
sensor fault. Turn the machine off and on once. If the light still blinks,
contact support with your order number, as the machine may need a warranty
repair.
